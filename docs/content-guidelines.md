# Milan Red Line Content Guidelines v1

## Mission

Help travellers make smarter accommodation decisions before they book.

We don't write to sell apartments.

We write to solve real travel questions.

If readers trust our advice, bookings become a natural consequence.

---

## Editorial Principles

### 1. Help first

Every guide must answer a real question.

Not promote the apartment.

Examples:

- Where to stay without a car
- Is Sesto San Giovanni a good place to stay?
- Where to stay for Carroponte concerts?

Never:

> Our apartment is the best.

Instead:

> Here is how to choose.

---

### 2. Honest recommendations

If another area is genuinely better for a specific type of traveller, say so.

Example:

If someone wants nightlife until 3 AM…

Recommend Navigli.

This builds trust.

---

### 3. Transport before geography

Our biggest differentiator.

We don't recommend neighbourhoods.

We recommend transport connections.

The right metro line is often more important than staying in the historic centre.

---

### 4. Local knowledge

Every article should contain information that generic travel blogs usually don't mention.

Examples:

- local supermarkets
- railway connections
- neighbourhood atmosphere
- everyday convenience
- practical advice

---

## Writing Style

- Friendly.
- Professional.
- Never exaggerated.
- Never "salesy".
- Short paragraphs.
- Simple English.
- No unnecessary adjectives.

Good:

> The metro station is a five-minute walk away.

Bad:

> The amazingly located apartment offers an unforgettable experience.

---

## Standard Article Structure

Every guide follows the same structure.

### Hero

Title

Subtitle

---

### Introduction

Answer the user's question immediately.

---

### Main sections

4–8 sections.

One idea per section.

---

### Comparison

Whenever possible:

- table
- bullets
- comparison

Readers love comparisons.

---

### Local Tip

Every guide includes one practical tip.

Example:

> Buy a 24-hour ATM ticket instead of driving.

---

### FAQ

3–5 questions.

Real questions.

Not invented keywords.

---

### Soft CTA

Always at the end.

Never aggressive.

Example:

> Looking for a comfortable apartment near the M1 Red Line?

---

## Internal Linking

Every guide should link naturally to:

- another guide
- one apartment page

Never force links.

---

## SEO Philosophy

We write for people.

SEO follows naturally.

Never repeat keywords unnaturally.

Instead:

- answer questions;
- explain clearly;
- use descriptive headings.

---

## Tone

Imagine helping a friend plan their first trip to Milan.

That's the tone.
