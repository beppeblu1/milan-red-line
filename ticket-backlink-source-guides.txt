{/* rx:introduction */}

Arriving in Milan can feel complicated at first, but the city becomes much easier to understand once you identify the right connection to the metro.

Whether you arrive by plane or train, the simplest approach is to plan the complete journey to your accommodation before leaving the terminal or station.

If you'll be using public transport during your stay, our guide to [How to Use Milan Public Transport](/guides/how-to-use-milan-public-transport) explains how the metro, trams, buses and regional trains work together, making it much easier to plan your journeys around the city.
{/* rx:audience */}

## Who is this guide for?

This guide is useful if you are:

- arriving at Malpensa, Linate or Orio al Serio Airport;
- arriving by train at Milano Centrale;
- visiting Milan for the first time;
- travelling without a car;
- attending meetings, exhibitions or events;
- deciding where to stay before completing your journey.

For a more detailed look at travelling around the city without driving, read our guide to [where to stay in Milan without a car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car).

## Arriving by plane

Milan is served by three main airports. The best connection depends on where you are staying, how much luggage you have and when you arrive.

### Malpensa

Malpensa is the furthest of Milan's main airports, but it has established rail and coach connections towards the city.

Before boarding, check which Milan station or transport hub gives you the simplest onward journey. The fastest route to central Milan is not always the easiest route to your accommodation.

### Linate

Linate is the closest airport to the city and is connected to Milan's public transport network.

For many travellers, continuing by metro is simpler than taking a taxi, particularly when the accommodation is close to a station.

### Orio al Serio

Orio al Serio is located near Bergamo and is generally connected to Milan by airport coaches.

Most services arrive at a major transport hub, where you can continue by metro, train or local public transport.

<GuideHighlightCard
  icon="info"
  title="Airport arrival"
>
Choose the service that leaves you closest to the most useful metro or railway connection, not simply the one that reaches Milan first.
</GuideHighlightCard>

## Where should you stay?

The most practical area depends on the purpose of your trip.

### Tourism

First-time visitors usually benefit from accommodation near a direct metro line rather than focusing only on the historic centre.

Our guide to the [best areas to stay in Milan for first-time visitors](/guides/best-area-to-stay-in-milan-for-first-time-visitors) explains how to compare central and well-connected residential areas.

### Business

For work trips, predictable journeys matter more than being close to a single landmark.

Staying near the metro can simplify travel between meetings, railway stations and exhibition areas. Read our guide to the [best areas to stay in Milan for business travellers](/guides/best-area-to-stay-in-milan-for-business-travellers) for more detailed advice.

### Events and exhibitions

If you are travelling to **Rho Fiera Milano**, accommodation along the M1 can provide a direct connection while keeping the rest of Milan accessible.

Our dedicated guide explains [where to stay near Rho Fiera Milano](/guides/where-to-stay-near-rho-fiera-milano).

### Hospital visits

For hospital appointments or longer stays, prioritise a clear door-to-door journey, nearby pharmacies and supermarkets, and accommodation where you can rest comfortably.

## Arriving by train

Most international and long-distance trains arrive at **Milano Centrale**, one of the city's main transport hubs.

The station is large, but the next step is usually simple: follow the coloured signs towards the metro and identify the line and direction needed for your accommodation.

### Before you leave the station

Take a few minutes to:

- buy or validate the correct travel ticket;
- follow the colour-coded metro signs;
- check the complete route, including changes;
- confirm the final walking section;
- compare public transport with a taxi before choosing automatically.

Once you understand the nearest metro station to your accommodation, the rest of the journey becomes much easier.

## Why the metro matters

Milan's metro connects the historic centre, major railway stations, business districts, exhibition areas and many residential neighbourhoods.

A direct line can be more useful than a central address, especially when you are carrying luggage or arriving after a long journey.

The **M1 Red Line** connects destinations such as Duomo, Cadorna and Rho Fiera Milano. Our guide to [where to stay near the M1 Red Line](/guides/where-to-stay-near-the-m1-red-line) explains why it is one of the most useful lines for visitors.

## Beyond Milan

Once you've settled in, Milan becomes an excellent starting point for exploring other destinations across Lombardy.

From Sesto San Giovanni you can easily continue towards:

- **Monza**, for the Royal Villa, the park and Monza Circuit;
- **Lake Como**, using regional railway services;
- **Lecco** and other lakeside destinations;
- many towns across northern Lombardy.

Knowing which metro line to take, where to change and how you'll reach your accommodation usually saves more time than trying to decide once you've arrived.

## Frequently asked questions

### Which airport is closest to Milan?

Linate is the closest airport to the city, while Malpensa and Orio al Serio are located further away and require a longer transfer.

### Is it better to take a taxi or the metro?

It depends on your destination. If your accommodation is close to a metro station, public transport is often the simplest and most predictable option.

### Is Milano Centrale connected to the metro?

Yes. Milano Centrale is directly connected to Milan's metro network, making it easy to continue towards most areas of the city.

### Where should first-time visitors stay?

For many travellers, staying close to a direct metro line is more practical than choosing accommodation based only on proximity to the Duomo.

Our guide to the [best areas to stay in Milan for first-time visitors](/guides/best-area-to-stay-in-milan-for-first-time-visitors) explains the different options.

### Can I visit Milan without a car?

Absolutely. Most visitors rely entirely on public transport throughout their stay.

Our guide to [where to stay in Milan without a car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) explains why choosing the right location often matters more than driving.

## Understanding Milan's transport network

Milan's transport system is fully integrated. Metro, trams, buses and regional trains work together, so you don't need to choose only one type of transport.

### Metro

The metro is the fastest option for most visitors. It connects the city centre, major attractions, railway stations and business districts.

### Trams

Trams are ideal for shorter urban journeys and neighbourhoods that are not directly served by the metro.

### Buses

Buses complete the network and are often useful for the final stretch after leaving the metro.

### Regional trains

Regional trains connect Milan with destinations such as **Monza**, **Lecco** and **Lake Como**, making them useful for day trips as well as local travel.

## How to use the Milan Metro

The metro is the easiest way to explore Milan.

There are five lines, each serving different parts of the city.

### M1 Red Line

The **M1 Red Line** is the most useful line for many visitors. It connects **Duomo**, **Cadorna**, **Rho Fiera Milano** and **Sesto San Giovanni**.

If you're planning your accommodation, our guide to [Where to Stay Near the M1 Red Line](/guides/where-to-stay-near-the-m1-red-line) explains why this route is one of the most practical in Milan.

### M2 Green Line

Useful for **Milano Centrale**, **Cadorna** and several eastern and southern districts.

### M3 Yellow Line

Provides a direct north-south connection through Milan and serves both **Duomo** and **Milano Centrale**.

### M4 Blue Line

Connects **Linate Airport** with central Milan, making airport transfers much easier than in the past.

### M5 Purple Line

Serves northern Milan, including **Bicocca** and **San Siro**, making it useful for university visitors, concerts and football matches.

### Planning your journey

Before leaving, enter your destination into **Google Maps** or another reliable journey planner.

Always check:

- the nearest station;
- the direction of travel;
- whether you need to change lines;
- the final walking distance;
- any service updates before travelling.

### Contactless payments

Many visitors travel using a contactless bank card or digital wallet instead of buying paper tickets.

The system is straightforward, but you should always use the same payment method throughout your journey.

Detailed guidance about tickets, fares and contactless travel will be covered in a dedicated guide about **ATM tickets and contactless payments**.
