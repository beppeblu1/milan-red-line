# Where to Stay in Milan Without a Car

The smartest areas to stay if you want to explore Milan using public transport instead of driving.

Visiting Milan without a car is not only possible — for most travellers, it's actually the smartest choice.

The city has one of Italy's best public transport systems, with four metro lines, an extensive tram network and regional trains connecting Milan with nearby destinations such as Monza, Lake Como and Lecco.

Many first-time visitors assume they need to stay in the historic centre to enjoy the city. In reality, choosing accommodation close to the right metro line is often far more important than choosing the most central location.

A well-connected neighbourhood can offer a quieter environment, better value for money and faster journeys to Milan's main attractions than many hotels located in the city centre.

---

## Can you visit Milan without a car?

Absolutely.

Unless you're planning to explore the countryside every day, a car is more likely to create problems than solve them.

Driving in Milan means dealing with:

- Heavy traffic during peak hours.

- Expensive parking.

- Restricted traffic zones (ZTL).

- Busy streets that can be stressful if you don't know the city.

Public transport, on the other hand, is designed to make getting around simple.

The metro reaches most of Milan's main attractions, while buses, trams and regional trains complete an excellent transport network.

For visitors staying a few days, purchasing an ATM travel pass is usually cheaper and far less stressful than renting a car.

---

## The biggest mistake first-time visitors make

Many visitors believe that staying close to the Duomo automatically guarantees the best experience.

While the historic centre is beautiful, it's not always the most practical place to stay.

Accommodation is often more expensive, streets are busier and rooms tend to be smaller.

Instead of asking:

> "How close is this hotel to the Duomo?"

a better question is:

> **"How quickly can I reach the places I want to visit?"**

Choosing accommodation close to a direct metro line often saves more time than staying within walking distance of one attraction.

This is why many experienced travellers prefer well-connected residential areas over the historic centre.

## Why Sesto San Giovanni is a smart choice

There is a reason many visitors choose to stay just outside Milan rather than directly in the city centre.

Sesto San Giovanni combines excellent public transport with a quieter residential atmosphere.

Here you'll find:

- Direct access to the M1 Red Line.

- Supermarkets within walking distance.

- Restaurants and cafés nearby.

- Pharmacies and everyday services.

- Comfortable residential streets.

- Excellent railway connections across Lombardy.

Instead of returning to crowded tourist streets every evening, you can enjoy a quieter neighbourhood while remaining only a short metro ride from Milan's attractions.

## Local Tip

💡 **Choose the metro, not the postcode.**

A direct metro connection is often more valuable than staying within walking distance of the Duomo.

Buying a 24-hour or 72-hour ATM travel pass gives unlimited access to Milan's metro, buses and trams, usually costing far less than parking a car for a single day.

### Do I need a car to visit Milan?

No. For most visitors, Milan's public transport is faster, cheaper and more convenient.

Yes. Milan's metro is modern, reliable and connects the city's main attractions.
