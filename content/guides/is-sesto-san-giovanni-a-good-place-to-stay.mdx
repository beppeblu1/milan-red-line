Sesto San Giovanni is one of the most practical places to stay when visiting Milan.

Located just north of the city, it combines direct access to the **M1 Red Line**, a quieter residential atmosphere and excellent value compared with many central neighbourhoods.

For many visitors, a fast metro connection matters far more than staying within the historic centre.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Sesto San Giovanni part of Milan?

No. It is an independent municipality immediately north of Milan, but it is fully connected to the city through the M1 metro, regional trains and the road network.

### How long does it take to reach central Milan?

The journey to **Duomo** takes around 20 minutes on the **M1 Red Line**, depending on your departure station and waiting time.

### Is Sesto San Giovanni a good base for tourists?

Yes. It offers direct metro access, quieter surroundings and generally better value than many central neighbourhoods.

### Can I stay here without a car?

Absolutely. The metro and railway network make it easy to explore Milan and nearby destinations.

Our guide to [Where to Stay in Milan Without a Car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) explains why many visitors choose this approach.

### Is Sesto San Giovanni convenient for Rho Fiera Milano?

Yes. The **M1 Red Line** provides a direct connection, making the area a practical alternative for visitors attending exhibitions and conferences.

Read our guide to [Where to Stay Near Rho Fiera Milano](/guides/where-to-stay-near-rho-fiera-milano) for more detailed advice.

### Is it suitable for families?

Yes. Families often appreciate the quieter environment, larger apartments and easy access to supermarkets, parks and public transport.
