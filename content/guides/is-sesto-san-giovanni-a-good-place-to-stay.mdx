# Is Sesto San Giovanni a Good Place to Stay When Visiting Milan?

A local guide to one of the smartest areas for staying near Milan.

## Short answer

Yes. If you're looking for a comfortable, well-connected and more affordable alternative to staying in Milan city centre, Sesto San Giovanni is one of the smartest places to stay.

Thanks to the M1 Red Line, you can reach the heart of Milan in around 20 minutes while enjoying quieter surroundings, larger apartments and better value.

For many visitors, being close to a direct metro connection is more useful than paying extra to stay in the historic centre.

## Why travellers choose Sesto San Giovanni

Sesto San Giovanni is a practical base for different types of travellers.

Its main advantages include:

- A direct metro connection to central Milan.
- Larger accommodation for the same budget.
- Quieter residential surroundings.
- Supermarkets, pharmacies, cafés and restaurants within walking distance.
- Convenient connections for business trips and longer stays.
- A railway station for travelling beyond Milan.

The area works particularly well for visitors who plan to spend most of the day exploring Milan but prefer to return to a calmer neighbourhood in the evening.

## How long does it take to reach Milan?

The M1 Red Line connects Sesto San Giovanni directly with several important destinations.

| Destination | Approximate travel time |
| --- | ---: |
| Duomo | Around 20 minutes |
| San Babila | Around 22 minutes |
| Cadorna | Around 25 minutes |
| Rho Fiera Milano | Around 35 minutes |

Actual travel times depend on the departure station, waiting time and any connections required.

The main advantage is simplicity: many central destinations can be reached directly on the M1 without complicated changes.

If you'd like to understand why this metro line is so useful for visitors, our guide to [staying near the M1 Red Line](/guides/where-to-stay-near-the-m1-red-line) explains the main connections in more detail.

## Is Sesto San Giovanni safe?

Sesto San Giovanni is a lively residential city where thousands of people live and work every day.

As in any large urban area, it is sensible to take normal precautions, particularly late at night and around busy transport hubs.

Most visitors are pleasantly surprised by how practical and residential the area feels. Shops, restaurants, supermarkets and public transport are all close by, and many streets are considerably quieter than central Milan.

It would not be accurate to describe any urban area as completely risk-free, but Sesto San Giovanni is generally a normal, lived-in city rather than a tourist district.

## Is Sesto San Giovanni cheaper than central Milan?

Accommodation outside Milan's historic centre can often offer better value, particularly for travellers looking for:

- More space.
- A separate kitchen.
- A washing machine.
- Longer stays.
- Accommodation for families or small groups.

Prices naturally change according to the season, events and demand, but staying near a direct metro line can provide an effective balance between convenience and cost.

The key question is not simply how far the accommodation is from the Duomo, but how easily the metro connects you to the places you want to visit.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Sesto San Giovanni inside Milan?

Sesto San Giovanni is a separate municipality immediately north of Milan. It is part of the wider Milan metropolitan area and is directly connected to the city by metro, rail and road.

### How long does it take to reach the Duomo?

The journey typically takes around 20 minutes on the M1 Red Line, depending on the departure station and waiting time.

### Is Sesto San Giovanni a good base for tourists?

Yes, particularly for visitors who value direct metro access, quieter surroundings, more space and better value than many central alternatives.

### Can I stay in Sesto San Giovanni without a car?

Yes. The metro, buses and railway connections make it possible to visit Milan and several nearby destinations using public transport.
