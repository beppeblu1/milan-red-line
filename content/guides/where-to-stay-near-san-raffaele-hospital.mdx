## Why not stay next to the hospital?

San Raffaele Hospital is located in the eastern part of Milan and includes one of Italy's leading research hospitals together with **Vita-Salute San Raffaele University**.

Because the hospital is well connected by metro and local transport, many visitors choose accommodation outside the immediate area without making their daily journeys significantly more complicated.

When comparing different options, consider the complete journey rather than the map alone.

For longer stays, a quieter residential neighbourhood can often provide a more comfortable environment.

## Frequently asked questions

### Which metro station serves San Raffaele Hospital?

Most visitors travel to **Cascina Gobba** on the M2 Green Line before continuing to the hospital using the dedicated light metro or ATM bus 925.

### Can I reach San Raffaele Hospital without a car?

Yes. The hospital is well connected by Milan's public transport network and can be reached using the metro together with local connecting services.

If you're travelling entirely by public transport, our guide to [where to stay in Milan without a car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) includes additional practical advice.

### Is an apartment suitable for a longer hospital stay?

For many visitors, yes. An apartment often provides a kitchen, washing machine, more living space and greater flexibility than a traditional hotel room.

### Should I stay in central Milan?

Not necessarily. A residential area with reliable public transport may offer more comfort, quieter surroundings and better everyday services while remaining easy to reach.

### Can I reach San Raffaele Hospital from Linate Airport?

Yes. Public transport connections, including ATM bus services, make travelling from Linate straightforward. Before travelling, check the latest routes and timetables.
