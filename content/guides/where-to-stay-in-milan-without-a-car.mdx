Exploring Milan without a car is not only possible—it is often the easiest choice.

With an extensive network of metro lines, trams, buses and regional trains, most visitors can reach the city's main attractions without driving. Choosing accommodation with good public transport is usually more important than staying in the historic centre.

For a practical introduction to the network, read our guide on [how to use Milan public transport](/guides/how-to-use-milan-public-transport).

## What really matters when choosing accommodation

When comparing different areas, look beyond the postcode.

A practical location should offer:

- a metro station within walking distance;
- supermarkets and pharmacies nearby;
- cafés and restaurants;
- easy access to the places you plan to visit;
- railway connections if you intend to explore beyond Milan.

For many visitors, a residential neighbourhood with excellent transport links provides a better overall experience than the busiest central districts.

## Why consider Sesto San Giovanni?

Sesto San Giovanni combines direct metro access with a quieter residential atmosphere.

Visitors can easily reach central Milan while enjoying supermarkets, cafés, restaurants and everyday services within walking distance.

If you're still deciding whether the area is right for your trip, our guide to [Is Sesto San Giovanni a Good Place to Stay?](/guides/is-sesto-san-giovanni-a-good-place-to-stay) explains its advantages in more detail.

## More than Milan

Staying in Sesto San Giovanni is also convenient if you plan to explore beyond Milan.

Regional trains provide straightforward connections to destinations including:

- **Monza**, for the Royal Villa, its park and the Formula 1 circuit;
- **Lecco**, for lakeside walks and mountain scenery;
- **Lake Como**, via regional rail services.

If you're attending an event at the Autodromo, our guide to [Where to Stay Near Monza Circuit](/guides/where-to-stay-near-monza-circuit) can help you plan your visit.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need a car to visit Milan?

No. For most visitors, Milan's public transport network is faster, cheaper and easier than driving.

### Which metro line is best for tourists?

The **M1 Red Line** is one of the most useful because it connects Duomo, San Babila, Cadorna and Rho Fiera Milano.

### Is Sesto San Giovanni a good place to stay without a car?

Yes. It offers direct metro access, railway connections and all the everyday services most travellers need.

### Can I visit Rho Fiera Milano without a car?

Yes. The M1 provides a direct connection, making it a practical option for exhibitions and conferences.

Our guide to [Where to Stay Near Rho Fiera Milano](/guides/where-to-stay-near-rho-fiera-milano) explains the best accommodation strategy.

### Can I visit Lake Como by train?

Yes. Regional trains make several destinations around Lake Como accessible without renting a car.
