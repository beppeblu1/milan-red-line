# Where to Stay in Milan Without a Car

The smartest areas to stay if you want to explore Milan using public transport instead of driving.

Visiting Milan without a car is not only possible — for most travellers, it is actually the simplest and most convenient choice.

The city has an extensive public transport network made up of metro lines, trams, buses and regional trains. Driving often means dealing with traffic, restricted traffic zones and expensive parking, while public transport connects most of the places visitors want to reach.

## Can you visit Milan without a car?

Absolutely.

For most travellers, public transport is faster, cheaper and less stressful than driving.

Milan's transport network connects the main attractions, business districts, railway stations and exhibition areas. Visitors staying close to a metro station can move around the city easily without renting a car.

## The biggest mistake first-time visitors make

Many visitors assume they must stay close to the Duomo to enjoy Milan.

A better question is not how close an apartment is to one attraction, but how quickly it connects you to all the places you want to visit.

Accommodation close to a direct metro line can provide:

- Easier journeys.
- Quieter evenings.
- More space.
- Better value.
- Less dependence on taxis.

The quality of the transport connection is often more important than the postcode itself.

## What really matters when choosing accommodation

Look for accommodation with:

- A metro station within walking distance.
- Supermarkets and pharmacies nearby.
- Restaurants and cafés in the neighbourhood.
- A railway station if you plan day trips.
- A simple route to the places you want to visit.

A well-connected residential area can be more practical than a central address that requires several changes or long walks.

## Why Sesto San Giovanni works well without a car

Sesto San Giovanni combines direct metro access with a quieter residential atmosphere.

Visitors can usually find:

- Supermarkets.
- Pharmacies.
- Restaurants and cafés.
- Everyday services.
- Metro and bus stops.
- Railway connections.

This allows guests to enjoy more space and calmer evenings while remaining closely connected to Milan city centre.

## More than Milan: easy day trips by train

The nearby railway station makes it possible to explore other destinations without renting a car.

### Monza

Monza is an easy option for visiting the Royal Villa, its large park and the famous Formula 1 circuit.

### Lecco

Lecco offers lakeside walks, mountain scenery and a more relaxed atmosphere than some of the busiest tourist destinations.

### Lake Como

Regional railway connections make several destinations around Lake Como accessible for a day trip, although routes and changes should always be checked for the chosen travel date.

This makes Sesto San Giovanni a useful base for exploring both Milan and other parts of Lombardy.

## Local tip

> Choose the metro connection, not only the postcode.

A 24-hour or 72-hour ATM travel pass can provide unlimited travel on Milan's metro, buses and trams and is usually far cheaper than driving and parking in the city.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need a car to visit Milan?

For most visitors, no. Public transport is normally faster, cheaper and more convenient.

### Is Milan's metro easy to use?

Yes. The network is clearly signposted and connects the city's main attractions.

### Which metro line is useful for tourists?

The M1 Red Line is particularly convenient because it connects Duomo, San Babila, Cadorna and Rho Fiera Milano.

### Can I visit Lake Como without a car?

Yes. Regional railway connections make several lakeside destinations accessible by public transport.

### Is staying outside the city centre a disadvantage?

Not necessarily. A well-connected residential area can offer better value, more space and equally convenient access to Milan.
