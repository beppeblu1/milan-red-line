# Best Area to Stay in Milan for First-Time Visitors

Planning your first trip to Milan can be exciting, but deciding where to stay isn't always straightforward.

Many visitors assume they need to book accommodation in the historic city centre. While that can be a good option, it's not the only one.

Choosing a neighbourhood with excellent public transport can make it just as easy to explore Milan while often offering a quieter atmosphere, more space and better value.

{/* rx:audience */}

## Who is this guide for?

This guide may be helpful if you're:

- Visiting Milan for the first time.
- Planning a city break.
- Travelling as a couple or family.
- Looking for an apartment instead of a hotel.
- Unsure which area best suits your trip.

{/* rx:key-points */}

## What first-time visitors usually want

Most people visiting Milan for the first time hope to experience the city's best-known attractions without spending too much time travelling.

A convenient location usually offers:

- Easy access to the metro.
- Good connections to the main attractions.
- Restaurants and cafés nearby.
- Supermarkets and everyday services.
- A comfortable place to relax after a day of sightseeing.

Choosing the right area can make your entire stay feel more relaxed.

{/* rx:area-selection */}

## Which areas should you consider?

The best area depends on the type of trip you're planning.

If you want to spend most of your time exploring the historic centre, neighbourhoods with direct metro access are often the most practical choice.

If you also plan to visit places outside the city centre, attend events or take day trips, staying in a well-connected residential area can offer greater flexibility.

Rather than focusing only on a central postcode, think about how easily you can reach the places you actually want to visit.

{/* rx:transport */}

## Why transport connections matter

Milan has one of Italy's best public transport networks.

For many visitors, choosing accommodation close to a metro station makes getting around simple from the first day. Our guide to [staying in Milan without a car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) explains what to look for when comparing different areas.

A direct journey is often easier than staying closer to the city centre but needing several transport changes.

Staying near the [M1 Red Line](/guides/where-to-stay-near-the-m1-red-line), for example, provides direct access to several places that first-time visitors commonly want to reach.

Good transport links can make your stay smoother, leaving you more time to enjoy the city.

## Why Sesto San Giovanni is worth considering

For first-time visitors, Sesto San Giovanni offers a practical combination of comfort and convenience.

Located on the M1 Red Line, it provides direct access to many of Milan's best-known destinations while offering a quieter residential atmosphere than the busiest parts of the city.

Many travellers appreciate staying in an area where everyday services are easy to reach.

You'll find:

- Supermarkets.
- Pharmacies.
- Cafés.
- Restaurants.
- Bakeries.
- Local shops.

For visitors spending several days in Milan, this can make everyday life much easier.

{/* rx:day-trips */}

## Beyond the city centre

Your first visit to Milan doesn't have to be limited to the historic centre.

Staying in a well-connected area also makes it easy to discover places beyond the city's main attractions.

From Sesto San Giovanni, you can conveniently visit:

- Monza.
- The Royal Villa of Monza.
- Lake Como by regional train.
- Other destinations across Lombardy.

Many travellers find that combining Milan with a day trip makes their visit even more memorable.

## Local tip

> Don't try to see everything in one day.

Milan is a city best enjoyed at a comfortable pace. Choosing accommodation with good transport connections allows you to return easily for a break before heading out again in the evening.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is the city centre the best place to stay in Milan?

Not always. While the historic centre is convenient for sightseeing, many well-connected residential areas offer an equally practical base with a quieter atmosphere.

### Is Sesto San Giovanni a good choice for first-time visitors?

For many travellers, yes. It offers direct metro access to central Milan together with a wide range of everyday services.

### Can I visit Milan without a car?

Absolutely. Most visitors rely entirely on Milan's public transport network, which connects the city's main attractions efficiently.

### How many days should I spend in Milan?

Two or three days are enough to discover many of the city's highlights, while a longer stay allows time for day trips to places such as Monza or Lake Como.

### Is an apartment a good alternative to a hotel?

For many visitors, yes. Apartments often provide more space, greater flexibility and a more relaxed experience, especially for stays of several nights.
