# How to Visit Monza from Milan

Monza is one of the easiest and most rewarding day trips you can take while staying in Milan.

Although it's internationally famous for the Formula 1 Italian Grand Prix, Monza has much more to offer than its legendary racing circuit. Its historic centre, elegant squares, Royal Villa and one of Europe's largest enclosed parks make it an enjoyable destination throughout the year.

Thanks to frequent train connections, visiting Monza doesn't require a car or a complicated itinerary. For many travellers, it's the perfect way to experience a different side of Lombardy without travelling far from Milan.

This guide explains how to reach Monza, what to see and how to make the most of your day.

## What makes Monza worth visiting?

Monza offers a combination of history, culture and green spaces that surprises many first-time visitors.

Unlike larger tourist destinations, it has a relaxed atmosphere that makes it easy to explore on foot.

Most visitors spend their time between three main attractions.

### The historic centre

The old town is full of cafés, local restaurants and attractive streets that are ideal for a leisurely walk.

It's the perfect place to enjoy lunch, browse independent shops or simply experience everyday life outside central Milan.

### The Royal Villa of Monza

The Royal Villa is one of northern Italy's most elegant historic residences.

Surrounded by extensive gardens, it provides an excellent starting point for exploring the surrounding park.

### Monza Park

Monza Park is one of the largest enclosed parks in Europe.

Whether you're looking for a peaceful walk, a picnic or simply some time surrounded by nature, it offers a very different atmosphere from Milan's busy streets.

### Monza Circuit

Even outside race weekends, the Autodromo Nazionale Monza remains one of the city's best-known attractions.

Depending on the period of the year, visitors may be able to join guided tours or attend other motorsport events.

During major events such as the Formula 1 Italian Grand Prix, accommodation and public transport are usually much busier than normal, so planning ahead is recommended.

## How to get to Monza from Milan

Travelling to Monza is straightforward.

Frequent regional trains connect Milan with Monza throughout the day, making it one of the most accessible destinations in Lombardy.

Most journeys take around 10–15 minutes, depending on the service and departure station.

After arriving at Monza railway station, the historic centre can be reached on foot, while buses and local transport provide convenient connections to the Royal Villa, Monza Park and the circuit.

For most visitors, travelling by train is simpler than driving, especially during weekends or major events.

## Why Milan is a great base for visiting Monza

Many visitors automatically assume they should stay in Monza if they plan to spend a day there.

In practice, Milan is often a more flexible base.

Staying in Milan allows you to combine Monza with other destinations such as Lake Como, the city centre, museums, shopping districts and business areas without changing accommodation.

Sesto San Giovanni can be an especially practical choice thanks to its convenient connections to both Milan and Monza.

Alongside public transport, you'll also find supermarkets, restaurants, cafés and all the everyday services that make longer stays more comfortable.

## A simple Monza day-trip itinerary

A relaxed itinerary could look like this.

### Morning

Take an early train from Milan and arrive in Monza before the busiest part of the day.

Walk through the historic centre, enjoy a coffee in one of the local cafés and explore the streets around Piazza Roma.

### Late morning

Visit the Royal Villa and spend some time in its gardens before continuing into Monza Park.

### Lunch

Choose one of the restaurants or cafés in the historic centre or enjoy a relaxed lunch near the park.

### Afternoon

Spend the afternoon walking through Monza Park.

If available during your visit, consider joining a guided tour of the Autodromo Nazionale Monza or exploring the surrounding area.

### Evening

Return to Milan and enjoy dinner near your accommodation.

Keeping the itinerary simple allows enough time to appreciate Monza without feeling rushed.

## Local tip

> Monza deserves more than a quick visit to the circuit.

Many first-time visitors head straight to the Autodromo and miss the city's historic centre and Royal Villa.

Allow time to explore beyond the circuit and you'll discover a quieter, elegant city with a very different atmosphere from Milan.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can you visit Monza in one day?

Yes.

Most visitors can comfortably explore the historic centre, the Royal Villa and Monza Park during a day trip from Milan.

### Do I need a car to visit Monza?

No.

Frequent train services make Monza one of the easiest destinations to reach by public transport.

### How long does it take to reach Monza from Milan?

Most train journeys take around 10–15 minutes, depending on the service and departure station.

### Is Monza worth visiting if I'm not interested in Formula 1?

Absolutely.

The Royal Villa, Monza Park and the historic centre make Monza an attractive destination even for visitors with no interest in motorsport.

### Can I visit the Formula 1 circuit?

The Autodromo can be visited throughout much of the year, although access depends on events, maintenance and guided tour availability.

Always check the official website before travelling.

### Is Monza suitable for families?

Yes.

The large park, pedestrian areas and relaxed atmosphere make it an enjoyable destination for families as well as couples.

### Can I combine Monza with Milan on the same day?

Yes.

Because of the short journey time, many visitors spend the morning or afternoon in Monza and the rest of the day in Milan.
