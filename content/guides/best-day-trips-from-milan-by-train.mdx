# Best Day Trips from Milan by Train

One of the biggest advantages of staying in Milan is that the city is much more than a destination in its own right.

Thanks to its excellent railway network, Milan is also the perfect starting point for exploring some of northern Italy's most interesting places.

In less than an hour, you can swap busy city streets for lakeside promenades, historic town centres, royal villas or large green parks—all without renting a car.

For many travellers, choosing accommodation with good transport connections makes these day trips easier than staying closer to the destinations themselves.

This guide will help you decide which excursion best matches your interests and available time.

## Which destination is right for you?

Not every traveller is looking for the same experience.

Some visitors dream of spending the day beside one of Italy's most famous lakes.

Others prefer elegant historic towns, royal palaces or peaceful parks.

The good news is that you don't need to choose only one type of trip before arriving in Milan.

Using Milan as your base allows you to decide according to the weather, your available time and your interests.

### Lake Como

Lake Como is one of the most popular excursions from Milan.

Visitors come for the lakeside scenery, elegant villas, picturesque towns and ferry rides across the water.

It is ideal if you're looking for spectacular views and a slower pace.

**Best for:**

- First-time visitors.
- Couples.
- Photography.
- Scenic walks.
- Relaxing day trips.

### Monza

Monza offers a completely different experience.

Instead of dramatic scenery, you'll find elegant streets, the Royal Villa, one of Europe's largest enclosed parks and the famous Formula 1 circuit.

It works particularly well for visitors interested in architecture, history and green spaces.

**Best for:**

- Culture.
- Parks.
- Families.
- Formula 1 enthusiasts.
- Easy half-day or full-day trips.

→ Read our complete guide:

**[How to Visit Monza from Milan](/guides/how-to-visit-monza-from-milan)**

### Lecco

Lecco combines mountains and lake views in a quieter atmosphere than many parts of Lake Como.

It is a good choice for travellers looking for beautiful scenery without some of the busiest tourist crowds.

## Why Milan is the perfect base

Choosing where to stay is just as important as choosing where to visit.

Many travellers assume they should stay close to each destination they plan to explore. In reality, using Milan as your base is often the simpler option.

The city's excellent rail network allows you to reach many places in Lombardy without changing accommodation every night.

Instead of packing and moving between hotels, you can enjoy comfortable day trips and return to the same apartment each evening.

If you're planning to explore the region without driving, our guide to [Where to Stay in Milan Without a Car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) explains why choosing accommodation near good public transport can make your entire trip easier.

## How to choose the right day trip

Each destination offers a different experience.

Choose **Lake Como** if you want:

- Beautiful lakeside scenery.
- Ferry rides.
- Picturesque villages.
- A relaxed pace.

Choose **Monza** if you prefer:

- Historic architecture.
- Large parks.
- Royal residences.
- An easy train journey.

Choose **Lecco** if you're looking for:

- Mountain landscapes.
- A quieter lakeside atmosphere.
- Scenic walks.
- Fewer crowds.

If you're staying in Milan for several days, consider visiting more than one destination.

Because travel times are relatively short, each trip offers a completely different experience without requiring long journeys.

{/* rx:local-tip */}

## Local tip

> Don't try to visit too much in a single day.

One well-planned destination is usually far more enjoyable than rushing between several places.

Allow yourself time to explore, stop for lunch and simply enjoy the atmosphere.

You'll often remember the slower moments more than the number of places you visited.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I visit these destinations without a car?

Yes.

All of the destinations featured in this guide can be reached using public transport, making them excellent choices for visitors staying in Milan.

### Which is the easiest day trip from Milan?

Monza is generally the quickest and simplest.

Lake Como is also very easy to organise, while Lecco offers a slightly longer but equally rewarding journey.

### Is one day enough?

For most visitors, yes.

Each destination can comfortably be explored in a full day, although many travellers choose to return for a longer visit in the future.

### Which destination is best for families?

Both Monza and Lake Como work well for families.

The best choice depends on whether you prefer parks and open spaces or lakeside scenery.

### Can I visit more than one destination during my stay?

Absolutely.

Many visitors spend several days in Milan and include two or three day trips during the same holiday.
