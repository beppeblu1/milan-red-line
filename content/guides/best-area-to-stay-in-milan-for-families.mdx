Travelling with children changes the way you choose accommodation.

For most families, everyday convenience matters more than being within walking distance of a famous landmark. Easy public transport, supermarkets, quieter streets and enough space often make a much bigger difference than staying in the historic centre.

This guide is designed for:

- families visiting Milan with children;
- parents travelling with babies or toddlers;
- visitors looking for apartments rather than hotels;
- travellers planning to explore Milan by public transport;
- families combining Milan with day trips across Lombardy.

## What families should look for

When comparing different neighbourhoods, focus on practical aspects that make each day easier.

Look for accommodation with:

- a metro station within walking distance;
- supermarkets and pharmacies nearby;
- restaurants suitable for families;
- quiet residential surroundings;
- enough space for everyone;
- a fully equipped kitchen if you're staying several days.

These everyday features usually have a greater impact on family travel than simply being closer to the Duomo.

## Choosing the right area

Different parts of Milan suit different travel styles.

### Historic centre

Ideal if your priority is walking to major attractions, although accommodation is often smaller, busier and more expensive.

### Brera

A beautiful district with excellent restaurants and cultural attractions, but generally one of the city's most expensive areas.

### Porta Nuova

Modern, well connected and close to pedestrian areas and parks, making it attractive for short family stays.

### Sesto San Giovanni

Families looking for more space, quieter evenings and direct access to the M1 often appreciate Sesto San Giovanni.

Instead of spending the entire day in crowded tourist districts, many visitors prefer returning to a residential neighbourhood after sightseeing.

Our guide to [Is Sesto San Giovanni a Good Place to Stay When Visiting Milan?](/guides/is-sesto-san-giovanni-a-good-place-to-stay) explains why it has become a popular base for many travellers.

## Getting around with children

Milan's public transport network makes it possible to explore the city comfortably without a car.

The M1 Red Line is particularly useful because it connects several destinations families commonly visit.

| Destination | Approximate travel time |
| --- | ---: |
| Duomo | Around 20 minutes |
| San Babila | Around 22 minutes |
| Cadorna | Around 25 minutes |
| Rho Fiera Milano | Around 35 minutes |

For many families, avoiding city traffic and expensive parking makes public transport the easiest option.

## Beyond Milan

A family holiday doesn't have to end in the city centre.

From Sesto San Giovanni, regional railway connections make it easy to explore destinations across Lombardy without renting a car.

Popular options include:

- **Monza**, with its Royal Villa, large park and family-friendly green spaces;
- **Lake Como**, for a relaxing day by the lake;
- **Lecco**, combining lakeside walks with mountain scenery.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Milan a good destination for families?

Yes. Milan offers parks, museums, pedestrian areas and an excellent public transport network that makes travelling with children straightforward.

### Do families need a car?

Usually not. Most visitors find the metro, trams and trains more convenient than driving and parking in the city.

### Is Sesto San Giovanni suitable for families?

For many families, yes. It combines direct metro access, everyday services, quieter surroundings and larger accommodation than many central districts.

### Is the metro easy to use with a stroller?

Many stations provide lifts or step-free access, although facilities vary. It's always worth checking the stations you'll use most before travelling.

### Is an apartment better than a hotel for families?

For longer stays, many families appreciate having a kitchen, separate living areas and more space than a typical hotel room.
