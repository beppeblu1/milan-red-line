# Best Area to Stay in Milan for Families

Practical advice on transport, space and choosing a comfortable base for exploring Milan with children.

Travelling with children changes the way you choose accommodation.

Instead of focusing only on famous landmarks, families often need more space, quieter surroundings and easy public transport.

Milan offers several good options for families, and staying just outside the historic centre can often provide a more comfortable experience.

## Is Milan a good destination for families?

Absolutely.

Although Milan is best known for fashion and business, it is also a surprisingly family-friendly city.

Visitors can enjoy:

- Public parks.
- Museums suitable for children.
- Pedestrian areas.
- Efficient public transport.
- Easy day trips to nearby destinations.

Families staying close to a metro station can explore the city comfortably without needing a car.

If you're planning to rely on public transport throughout your stay, our guide to [visiting Milan without a car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) offers practical advice for choosing the right area.

## What families should really look for

When travelling with children, convenience often matters more than being close to one famous landmark.

Useful features include:

- A metro station within walking distance.
- A supermarket nearby.
- A pharmacy.
- Restaurants suitable for families.
- Quiet surroundings.
- A fully equipped kitchen.
- Enough space for everyone.

These practical details can have a greater impact on the stay than being a few minutes closer to the Duomo.

## Choosing the right area

Different areas suit different kinds of family trips.

### Historic centre

The centre works well for families who want to walk to major attractions and do not mind higher prices, crowds and smaller accommodation.

### Brera

Brera offers attractive streets, culture and good public transport, but accommodation can be expensive.

### Porta Nuova

Porta Nuova is modern, well connected and close to parks and pedestrian areas, although prices are generally higher.

### Sesto San Giovanni

Sesto San Giovanni offers a quieter residential atmosphere, direct access to the M1 Red Line and apartments that can provide more space and flexibility.

You can also read our guide on [why many visitors choose Sesto San Giovanni](/guides/is-sesto-san-giovanni-a-good-place-to-stay) to understand what makes the area a practical base for exploring Milan.

The best area depends on your priorities. Families who value space, transport and everyday services may prefer a well-connected residential neighbourhood.

## Why many families choose Sesto San Giovanni

Sesto San Giovanni offers several advantages for families visiting Milan.

These include:

- Larger apartments.
- Direct access to the M1 Red Line.
- Supermarkets and pharmacies nearby.
- Local restaurants and cafés.
- Quieter residential streets.
- Fast access to central Milan.

Many families appreciate returning to calmer surroundings after spending the day exploring the city.

The journey to the Duomo usually takes around 20 minutes, depending on the departure station and waiting time.

## Why an apartment can work well for families

An apartment often gives families more flexibility than a traditional hotel room.

A kitchen makes it easier to prepare:

- Breakfast.
- Snacks.
- Simple meals.
- Food for young children.

A washing machine can be particularly useful during longer stays.

Separate sleeping and living areas can also make evenings more comfortable after children have gone to bed.

## Things to do with children in Milan

Milan offers many attractions suitable for families.

### Duomo

Children often enjoy the cathedral terraces and the views across the city.

### Castello Sforzesco

The castle, its museums and the surrounding pedestrian areas are easy to explore as a family.

### Parco Sempione

A useful place for children to run and relax between visits.

### MUBA

The children's museum offers exhibitions and activities designed for younger visitors.

### CityLife Park

A modern pedestrian area with open spaces, shops and places to stop for a break.

### Monza Park

One of Europe's largest enclosed parks and a good option for a day outside the city centre.

## Travelling with a stroller

Many metro stations provide lifts or step-free access, but facilities are not identical at every station.

Before travelling, check accessibility information for the stations you expect to use most frequently.

It can also help to:

- Avoid peak commuting hours.
- Use lifts where available.
- Allow extra time for changes.
- Keep a lightweight stroller if possible.
- Check whether attractions have dedicated family entrances.

## Everyday services that make family travel easier

Families often benefit from accommodation close to:

- Parks.

These services can be especially valuable during longer stays or when children need a break from sightseeing.

> Choose everyday convenience, not only distance from the Duomo.

Families often enjoy staying slightly outside the historic centre.

More space, quieter evenings and nearby supermarkets can make travelling with children more relaxing than staying in the busiest tourist areas.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Milan suitable for children?

Yes. Milan offers parks, museums, pedestrian areas and an extensive public transport network.

### Is the metro easy to use with a stroller?

Many stations provide lifts or step-free access, but it is advisable to check the specific stations before travelling.

### Is Sesto San Giovanni family friendly?

Its residential atmosphere, larger apartments and direct metro connection make it a practical choice for many families.

### How long does it take to reach the Duomo?

The journey usually takes around 20 minutes on the M1 Red Line, depending on the departure station and waiting time.

### Do families need a car in Milan?

For most visitors, no. Public transport is generally more convenient than driving and parking in the city.
