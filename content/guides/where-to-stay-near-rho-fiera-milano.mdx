Visiting **Rho Fiera Milano** for a trade fair, exhibition or conference? Choosing accommodation with fast and reliable transport can make your stay much easier.

Rather than staying next to the exhibition centre, many visitors prefer locations that offer direct metro connections, quieter surroundings and easy access to Milan itself.

Whether you're attending a one-day event or a week-long exhibition, choosing the right location can save time every day while giving you more flexibility outside exhibition hours.

This guide explains the best options for reaching **Rho Fiera Milano** while enjoying a comfortable stay.

## Choosing the right option

There are several areas where you can stay when visiting **Rho Fiera Milano**, each offering a different balance between convenience, atmosphere and price.

Hotels immediately around the exhibition centre are convenient if your only priority is walking to the venue. However, they often have limited availability during major events and prices can increase significantly.

Staying along the **M1 Red Line** offers a more flexible alternative. Areas such as **Sesto San Giovanni** provide direct metro access to Rho Fiera Milano while also giving you easy connections to Milan city centre, restaurants and everyday services.

If your visit includes both business and leisure, choosing accommodation on the M1 allows you to move easily between the exhibition centre and the rest of the city without changing transport.

## Getting there

One of the main advantages of [staying near the M1 Red Line](/guides/where-to-stay-near-the-m1-red-line) is the direct connection to **Rho Fiera Milano**.

There is no need to change trains or navigate multiple transport systems. Simply board the M1 towards **Rho Fiera** and travel directly to the exhibition centre.

This makes the journey predictable and convenient, especially during busy exhibition days when avoiding unnecessary transfers can save both time and stress.

<GuideHighlightCard
  icon="train"

If your accommodation is also close to a railway station, such as **Sesto San Giovanni FS**, you benefit from even greater flexibility for travelling to other parts of Milan and Northern Italy.

## Beyond the exhibition

If your visit to **Rho Fiera Milano** lasts several days, you may have some free time before or after the event.

Thanks to the excellent transport connections, staying on the M1 Red Line makes it easy to spend an evening in central Milan, visit neighbourhoods such as Brera or Porta Nuova, or even plan trips to destinations such as **Monza** or **Lake Como**.

If your schedule allows, you can also use your stay as an opportunity to explore other destinations across Northern Italy using Milan's excellent rail network.

This flexibility is one of the main advantages of choosing accommodation connected to Milan's public transport network rather than staying exclusively near the exhibition centre.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Sesto San Giovanni a good place to stay for Rho Fiera Milano?

Yes. The M1 Red Line provides a direct connection to Rho Fiera Milano, making Sesto San Giovanni a convenient base for many business travellers.

If you'd like a more detailed overview, we've prepared a dedicated guide explaining [whether Sesto San Giovanni is a good place to stay](/guides/is-sesto-san-giovanni-a-good-place-to-stay).

### How long does it take to reach Rho Fiera Milano?

The journey from **Sesto Rondò** takes approximately **30–35 minutes** on the M1 Red Line, without changing trains.

Travel times may vary slightly depending on the time of day and the event schedule.

### Is it better to stay near the exhibition centre?

Not necessarily.

Hotels close to the exhibition centre are convenient, but accommodation along the M1 often offers better value, more restaurants, additional services and much easier access to Milan city centre.

### Can I visit Milan after the exhibition?

Absolutely.

The M1 connects Rho Fiera Milano directly with central Milan, making it easy to enjoy restaurants, attractions and evening activities after your event.

### Good to know

Travel times may vary depending on the exhibition schedule, metro frequency and temporary service changes.

Before travelling, it's always worth checking:

- the official **ATM Milano** website for metro updates;
- the official **Rho Fiera Milano** calendar for exhibition dates and opening hours.

If you're attending one of the largest international exhibitions, booking accommodation well in advance is recommended, as hotels close to the exhibition centre often sell out quickly.

Choosing accommodation along the M1 Red Line can offer more flexibility while still providing straightforward access to the fairgrounds.
