# How to Visit Lake Como from Milan

Planning a trip to Lake Como is exciting, but deciding how to organise the visit is not always straightforward.

Many travellers assume they need to stay on the lake itself. While that can be a good choice for a longer holiday, it is not essential if you simply want to experience Lake Como during a trip to Milan.

Several lakeside towns can be reached by train, making it possible to leave Milan in the morning, spend most of the day by the water and return in the evening.

For many visitors, using Milan as a base offers more flexibility, a wider choice of accommodation and the opportunity to explore several destinations during the same stay.

This guide explains how to reach Lake Como, which town may suit you best and what to consider before travelling.

## Which Lake Como destination should you choose?

There is no single best destination for every visitor.

The right choice depends on how much time you have, how many transport changes you are comfortable making and what kind of experience you want.

### Como

Como is usually the simplest option for a first visit.

It offers a historic centre, a lakeside promenade, restaurants, shops and ferry connections to other towns.

Como may be the right choice if you want:

- A straightforward train journey.
- A mixture of town and lake views.
- Restaurants and services within easy reach.
- The option of taking a ferry.
- A relaxed day without a complicated itinerary.

It can also work well for families and visitors who prefer fewer transport changes.

### Varenna

Varenna offers a different experience.

It is smaller, quieter and often feels closer to the traditional image of a Lake Como village.

The railway station is within walking distance of the waterfront, although some routes include slopes and steps.

Varenna may suit you if you're looking for:

- A smaller lakeside village.
- Scenic waterfront walks.
- Ferry connections across the central lake.
- A quieter atmosphere than Como.
- A route that works well without a car.

It is also a useful arrival point for visitors continuing by ferry towards Bellagio or Menaggio.

### Bellagio

Bellagio is one of the best-known destinations on Lake Como, but it does not have its own railway station.

Most visitors reach it by combining a train journey with a ferry or bus.

One common option is to travel to Varenna and continue by ferry. Another is to reach Como and travel onward across the lake.

Bellagio can be visited in one day, but the journey requires more planning than a simple trip to Como or Varenna.

Choose Bellagio if it is one of the main reasons for your visit and you are comfortable coordinating train and ferry schedules.

For a first trip with limited time, Como or Varenna will usually be easier to organise.

## How to reach Lake Como from Milan

There is no single railway station called “Lake Como”.

The correct route depends on which town you want to visit.

| Destination | Typical departure area | Approximate journey |
| --- | --- | ---: |
| Como | Milano Centrale or Milano Cadorna | Around 40–60 minutes |
| Varenna | Milano Centrale | Around 60–70 minutes |
| Bellagio | Train to Como or Varenna, then ferry or bus | Longer combined journey |

These times are intended as general guidance.

The fastest or most convenient route may vary according to the departure station, train service and destination selected.

### Can you visit Lake Como in one day?

Yes.

For most visitors, a day trip is one of the easiest ways to experience Lake Como while staying in Milan.

Train services connect Milan with several towns around the lake. Depending on your route, you can enjoy a waterfront walk, explore a historic centre, take a ferry and return to Milan in the evening.

A day trip works particularly well if Lake Como is only one part of a wider itinerary.

If you want a slower holiday focused on villages, ferries and evenings by the lake, staying there for one or more nights may be more suitable.

### Do you need a car?

No.

For a straightforward visit to Como or Varenna, public transport is normally sufficient.

Travelling by train also avoids traffic, parking and restricted-access areas.

A car may provide more flexibility if you want to explore less accessible parts of the lake or stay for several days. However, it can make popular destinations more complicated during busy periods.

For most first-time visitors taking a day trip from Milan, the train is the simpler option.

## A simple Lake Como day-trip itinerary

A relaxed day may look like this.

### Morning

Leave Milan early enough to avoid rushing.

Travel by train to Como or Varenna and take some time to explore on foot after arriving.

### Late morning

Walk along the waterfront and visit the historic centre.

If you plan to use a ferry, check the departure point and schedule before moving too far from the station.

### Lunch

Have lunch near the lake, but allow enough time if you have chosen a specific return train or ferry.

Restaurants can become busy during weekends and the summer season.

### Afternoon

Continue exploring the town or take a short ferry journey if the timetable allows.

Avoid trying to include too many distant destinations in one day.

### Evening

Return to Milan and have dinner near your accommodation.

Keeping the itinerary simple usually creates a more enjoyable day than trying to visit every famous village at once.

### What should you check before leaving?

Before your trip:

- Check the complete outbound and return train journeys.
- Confirm the correct Milan departure station.
- Check ferry times separately from train times.
- Allow extra time between different transport services.
- Download or save tickets where possible.
- Check the weather forecast.
- Wear comfortable shoes.
- Keep an alternative return route available.

Do not assume that train and ferry timetables are coordinated.

A delayed arrival may mean waiting for the next available connection.

## Local tip

> Choose one main destination and leave some flexibility in your day.

Trying to visit Como, Bellagio and Varenna during the same short trip can make the day feel rushed.

For most first-time visitors, exploring one town properly and adding one short ferry journey creates a more relaxed experience.

Leaving early is also helpful during weekends and the summer season, when trains, ferries and lakeside towns can be much busier.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Lake Como worth visiting from Milan?

Yes. It is one of the most accessible day trips from Milan and offers a very different atmosphere from the city.

### How long does it take to reach Lake Como from Milan?

The journey usually takes around 40 to 70 minutes, depending on the destination, departure station and train service.

### Which Lake Como town is easiest to reach?

Como is generally one of the easiest choices because it has frequent railway connections and a wide range of services.

Varenna is also straightforward to reach by direct train from Milano Centrale on many routes.

### Can I visit Bellagio from Milan in one day?

Yes, but the journey normally requires a train followed by a ferry or bus. Check the complete itinerary before travelling.

### Is Como or Varenna better for a first visit?

Como is generally easier and offers more services. Varenna is smaller and more scenic. The better choice depends on the experience you prefer.

### Do I need to book train tickets in advance?

It depends on the train service. Some regional tickets can be purchased close to departure, while other services may have reserved seats or different conditions. Check the ticket rules for your chosen journey.

### Do I need to book ferry tickets?

Availability and ticketing arrangements vary by route and season. During busy periods, allow additional time and check the latest information before travelling.

### Can I visit Lake Como while staying in Sesto San Giovanni?

Yes. Sesto San Giovanni can be a practical base, although the best route depends on the destination and departure station selected.
