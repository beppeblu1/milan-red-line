Whether you're attending the **Formula 1 Italian Grand Prix**, a track day or another event at **Monza Circuit**, choosing the right place to stay can make your visit much more enjoyable.

Many visitors immediately search for accommodation in Monza itself. In practice, staying in a well-connected residential area nearby often provides greater flexibility, easier transport and the opportunity to explore both Monza and Milan during the same trip.

## Why not stay next to Monza Circuit?

The **Autodromo Nazionale Monza** is one of the world's most famous racing circuits and attracts visitors throughout the year.

During the Italian Grand Prix and other major events, accommodation close to the circuit is often limited and prices can increase significantly.

Many visitors therefore choose nearby residential areas that offer simpler transport, more accommodation choices and easier access to Milan.

Instead of looking only at the map, consider how practical your daily journeys will actually be.

## Why consider Sesto San Giovanni?

Sesto San Giovanni sits between Milan and Monza, making it a practical base for visitors planning to explore both destinations.

The area combines direct metro access, regional railway services and a quieter residential atmosphere than many central neighbourhoods.

If you're comparing different locations, our guide to [whether Sesto San Giovanni is a good place to stay](/guides/is-sesto-san-giovanni-a-good-place-to-stay) explains why many travellers choose it.

You'll typically find:

- supermarkets;
- pharmacies;
- cafés and restaurants;
- bakeries;
- local shops;
- everyday services.

## More than race weekend

Many visitors spend only part of their stay at the circuit and use the remaining time to explore the surrounding area.

From Sesto San Giovanni you can easily reach:

- Milan city centre;
- Monza;
- the Royal Villa of Monza;
- Monza Park;
- Lake Como and other destinations across Lombardy using regional trains.

This makes the area a practical base for visitors who want to enjoy more than the race weekend itself.

{/* rx:local-tip */}

## Local tip

> Plan for extra travel time on major event days.

Large crowds can affect both road traffic and public transport, especially during the Formula 1 Italian Grand Prix. Leaving earlier than planned usually makes the journey far more relaxed.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is it better to stay in Monza or Milan?

It depends on your plans. If you're only attending the event, Monza can be convenient. If you also want to explore Milan, a well-connected area between the two cities often offers greater flexibility.

### Is Sesto San Giovanni a good base for visiting Monza Circuit?

For many visitors, yes. It combines excellent transport connections with a quieter residential atmosphere while providing easy access to both Monza and Milan.

### Can I reach Monza Circuit without a car?

Absolutely. Public transport is widely used during major events and is often the easiest way to reach the circuit.

If you're planning your trip entirely by metro and train, our guide to [where to stay in Milan without a car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) provides additional practical advice.

### Is it worth staying several days?

Many visitors extend their stay to discover Milan, Monza and nearby destinations after attending the event.

### Is an apartment a good option for race weekend?

For visitors staying several nights or travelling with family or friends, an apartment often offers more space, flexibility and comfort than a standard hotel room.
