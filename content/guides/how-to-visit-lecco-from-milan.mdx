# How to Visit Lecco from Milan

Lecco is one of the most enjoyable day trips you can take from Milan.

Located on the south-eastern branch of Lake Como, the city combines lakeside views, mountain scenery and a relaxed atmosphere that feels very different from the pace of Milan.

Unlike some of the busiest destinations around the lake, Lecco offers a quieter experience while remaining easy to reach by train.

Whether you're looking for a scenic walk, a peaceful lunch by the water or simply a change of scenery, Lecco is an excellent choice for a day away from the city.

This guide explains how to get there, what to see and how to organise an enjoyable visit.

- A car is not necessary.

- The lakefront, historic centre and mountain views are the main attractions.
- Lecco is generally quieter than many other destinations around Lake Como.

## What makes Lecco worth visiting?

Lecco offers a different atmosphere from many of the better-known destinations around Lake Como.

Rather than focusing on elegant villas or luxury hotels, the city feels authentic and easy to explore.

Its compact historic centre, attractive waterfront and spectacular mountain backdrop make it ideal for visitors who simply want to enjoy a relaxed day outdoors.

### The lakefront

The promenade is one of Lecco's highlights.

Walking beside the lake with the mountains in the background is one of the simplest pleasures of visiting the city.

### Piazza XX Settembre

The heart of the historic centre is filled with cafés, restaurants and small shops.

It is an ideal place to stop for lunch or simply enjoy the atmosphere.

### Lungolago

The waterfront continues for a considerable distance and offers excellent views in every season.

Many visitors spend much of their day simply walking, taking photographs and enjoying the scenery.

### Mountain views

Unlike Como, Lecco is surrounded by dramatic peaks that create one of the most distinctive landscapes in northern Italy.

Even without hiking, the scenery is one of the city's greatest attractions.

## How to get to Lecco from Milan

Travelling to Lecco is straightforward.

Frequent regional trains connect Milan with Lecco throughout the day.

Most journeys take around 40 minutes, depending on the service and departure station.

The railway station is located within walking distance of the historic centre and the lakefront, making it easy to begin exploring immediately after arrival.

For most visitors, travelling by train is both easier and more relaxing than driving.

## Why Milan is a great base for visiting Lecco

Many visitors assume they should stay near the lake if they want to explore Lecco.

For a longer holiday, that may be the right choice.

However, if you're planning to combine several destinations during the same trip, Milan often provides much greater flexibility.

You can enjoy a full day in Lecco and still spend the rest of your stay exploring Milan, Monza or Lake Como without changing accommodation.

Sesto San Giovanni can be a particularly practical base thanks to its convenient connections to both central Milan and the regional rail network.

## A simple Lecco day-trip itinerary

A relaxed itinerary might look like this.

### Morning

Take an early train from Milan and arrive in Lecco before the waterfront becomes busier.

Start with a walk along the Lungolago and enjoy the changing views across the lake and surrounding mountains.

### Late morning

Explore the historic centre, browse the local shops and take your time discovering the quieter streets around Piazza XX Settembre.

### Lunch

Choose a lakeside restaurant or a traditional café in the centre.

One of the pleasures of visiting Lecco is slowing down and enjoying the atmosphere.

### Afternoon

Continue your walk along the waterfront or relax in one of the parks overlooking the lake.

If the weather is clear, the mountain scenery becomes even more impressive later in the day.

### Evening

Return to Milan by train and enjoy dinner near your accommodation.

A simple itinerary usually offers a much more enjoyable experience than trying to rush between several destinations.

## Local tip

> Don't spend your whole day checking your watch.

Lecco is one of those places that rewards a slower pace.

Leave time for an unplanned coffee, a walk along the waterfront or simply sitting on a bench overlooking the lake.

Sometimes the best part of the visit is not a specific attraction, but the atmosphere itself.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can you visit Lecco in one day?

Yes.

Lecco is compact enough to explore comfortably during a day trip from Milan.

### Do I need a car?

No.

Frequent regional trains make Lecco one of the easiest destinations to reach using public transport.

### How long does it take to reach Lecco from Milan?

Most train journeys take around 40 minutes, depending on the service and departure station.

### Is Lecco less crowded than Lake Como?

It can be.

While Lecco is part of the Lake Como area, it generally offers a quieter atmosphere than some of the most famous lakeside destinations, particularly outside peak travel periods.

### Is Lecco suitable for families?

Yes.

The lakeside promenade, open spaces and relaxed atmosphere make it a pleasant destination for families as well as couples.

### Can I combine Lecco with another destination on the same day?

It's possible, but many visitors find that Lecco deserves a full day on its own.

Travelling at a relaxed pace usually makes for a more enjoyable experience.
