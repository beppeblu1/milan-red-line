Whether you're visiting the **University of Milano-Bicocca** for a conference, graduation, research project or to see family and friends, choosing the right place to stay can make your trip much easier.

Many visitors automatically search for accommodation next to the campus. In practice, staying in a well-connected residential area often provides greater flexibility, quieter surroundings and convenient access to both the university and the rest of Milan.

{/* rx:audience */}

## Who is this guide for?

This guide is useful if you are:

- visiting a student, friend or family member;
- attending a conference, seminar or graduation;
- travelling for research or academic work;
- looking for an apartment rather than a hotel;
- planning to combine your university visit with time exploring Milan.

If you intend to rely on public transport during your stay, our guide to [where to stay in Milan without a car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) may also help.

## Key points

- Milano-Bicocca is well connected by metro, rail and bus.
- Staying next to the campus is not always the most convenient option.
- Sesto San Giovanni provides direct M1 access and excellent regional rail connections.
- Residential neighbourhoods often offer more space and quieter evenings.
- Public transport makes it easy to combine university visits with sightseeing.

## Why not stay next to the university?

The University of Milano-Bicocca is one of Italy's leading public universities and welcomes thousands of students, researchers and international visitors every year.

Because the campus is well served by public transport, many visitors choose accommodation outside the immediate university area without sacrificing convenience.

Rather than focusing only on distance, consider how easily you can reach the buildings you will actually visit and how practical the area will be after your daily activities.

## Why consider Sesto San Giovanni?

Sesto San Giovanni offers an excellent balance between transport, comfort and everyday services.

Visitors staying here benefit from direct access to the **M1 Red Line**, regional railway services and a residential atmosphere that many guests appreciate after a busy day.

If you're not familiar with the area, our guide to [whether Sesto San Giovanni is a good place to stay](/guides/is-sesto-san-giovanni-a-good-place-to-stay) explains why it has become a popular base for many visitors.

You'll typically find:

- supermarkets;
- pharmacies;
- cafés and restaurants;
- bakeries;
- local shops;
- useful everyday services.

## More than the university

If you're staying in Milan for several days, you'll probably want to explore beyond the campus.

From Sesto San Giovanni you can easily reach many of Milan's best-known destinations, including:

- Duomo;
- San Babila;
- Cadorna;
- CityLife;
- Rho Fiera Milano.

The nearby railway station also provides convenient connections towards **Monza**, **Lecco** and several destinations around **Lake Como**, making day trips easy without renting a car.

For visitors attending events beyond the university, this flexibility makes Sesto San Giovanni an excellent base for exploring both Milan and northern Lombardy.

## Local tip

> Choose the transport connection before choosing the postcode.

Spending a few minutes checking your route before booking can make your entire stay much easier.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is it necessary to stay next to Bicocca University?

No. Thanks to Milan's public transport network, many nearby residential areas provide convenient access to the campus while offering a wider range of accommodation.

### Is Sesto San Giovanni a good place to stay when visiting Bicocca?

For many visitors, yes. It combines excellent public transport, useful local services and a quieter residential atmosphere while remaining well connected to both the university and central Milan.

### Can I visit Bicocca University without a car?

Absolutely. Most visitors use the metro, regional trains and buses to reach the campus.

If you're planning your entire stay using public transport, our guide to [where to stay in Milan without a car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) provides additional advice.

### Is an apartment a good choice?

If you're staying for several nights or travelling with family, an apartment often provides more space, greater flexibility and a more comfortable experience than a traditional hotel room.

### Can I explore Milan during my stay?

Yes. Staying in a well-connected area makes it easy to combine university visits with sightseeing, shopping and other activities throughout the city.
