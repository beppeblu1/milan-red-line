Choosing accommodation near Milan's **M1 Red Line** can make your stay easier, whether you are visiting the city for sightseeing, business or an exhibition.

For many travellers, a direct metro connection is more useful than a central postcode. Fewer transfers, predictable journeys and access to a quieter neighbourhood can improve the entire stay.

To understand how the wider network works, read our practical guide to [using Milan public transport](/guides/how-to-use-milan-public-transport), including the metro, trams, buses and journey planning.

## Why choose accommodation near the M1 Red Line?

The M1 is one of Milan's most useful metro lines for visitors.

Staying within walking distance of a station makes journeys simpler and more predictable, especially when carrying luggage, travelling with children or following a fixed business schedule.

Instead of considering only the distance from the city centre, look at how easily you can reach the places you will actually visit.

A location outside the historic centre can still be highly convenient when the journey is direct.

## Why consider Sesto San Giovanni?

Sesto San Giovanni sits at the northern end of the M1 and offers a practical balance between transport, comfort and local services.

Visitors can reach central Milan directly while returning to a quieter residential area in the evening. Supermarkets, pharmacies, cafés, restaurants and local shops are available nearby.

If you are comparing locations, our guide on [whether Sesto San Giovanni is a good place to stay](/guides/is-sesto-san-giovanni-a-good-place-to-stay) provides an honest overview of the area and the travellers it suits best.

For practical information about supermarkets, food, local services, safety and everyday life, read our [Practical Guide to Sesto San Giovanni](/guides/practical-guide-to-sesto-san-giovanni).

The local railway station also adds flexibility for journeys towards Monza, Lecco and Lake Como.

## More than Milan

The M1 is only part of the transport network available from Sesto San Giovanni.

Regional trains make it possible to explore destinations beyond Milan without renting a car, including:

- **Monza**, for the Royal Villa, its park and the Formula 1 circuit;
- **Lecco**, for lakeside walks and mountain scenery;
- destinations around **Lake Como**, depending on the selected railway route.

If you are attending an event at the Autodromo, our guide to [where to stay near Monza Circuit](/guides/where-to-stay-near-monza-circuit) can help you compare transport and accommodation options.

For longer stays, these connections make Sesto San Giovanni a useful base for exploring more of Lombardy.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is the M1 Red Line good for tourists?

Yes. It connects several popular destinations, including Duomo, San Babila and Cadorna, and provides easy interchange with the rest of Milan's metro network.

### Is staying near the M1 better than staying in the city centre?

For many visitors, it can be. A direct metro connection may provide simpler journeys, quieter surroundings and better value than accommodation in the busiest central districts.

### Can I visit Milan without a car if I stay near the M1?

Yes. Public transport is sufficient for most visitors, and staying near the M1 makes many journeys straightforward.

Our guide to [where to stay in Milan without a car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) explains how to choose a practical location.

### Is Sesto San Giovanni connected to the M1?

Yes. Several M1 stations serve Sesto San Giovanni, providing direct access to central Milan and Rho Fiera Milano.

### Is the M1 useful for business travellers?

Yes. The line provides direct access to **Rho Fiera Milano**, making it convenient for exhibitions, conferences and business events.

For broader advice, read our guide to [the best areas to stay in Milan for business travellers](/guides/best-area-to-stay-in-milan-for-business-travellers).
