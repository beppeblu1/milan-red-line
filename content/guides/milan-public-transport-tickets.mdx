## Milan public transport tickets at a glance

Most visitors only need to consider three basic options:

- a standard ticket for occasional journeys;
- a daily or multi-day pass for frequent travel;
- contactless payment for travelling without a paper ticket.

The best choice depends mainly on how many journeys you expect to make, not simply on how many days you're staying in Milan.

### Standard ticket

A standard ticket is usually suitable for occasional journeys and flexible sightseeing days.

It can be the simplest option if you don't yet know exactly how often you'll use public transport.

### Daily or multi-day pass

A short-duration pass may be more convenient if you expect to travel several times during the same day or throughout a longer stay.

It allows you to move between attractions, restaurants and your accommodation without buying a separate ticket for every journey.

### Contactless payment

A compatible bank card or mobile wallet can often be used instead of purchasing a paper ticket.

This is particularly convenient for short stays and travellers who prefer not to queue at ticket machines.

## Which Milan public transport ticket should you buy?

Rather than memorising every fare, think about **how you plan to travel**.

### You're staying for a weekend

If you'll spend two or three days exploring Milan, you'll probably use the metro several times each day.

A daily or multi-day pass may be more convenient than purchasing individual tickets, especially if you expect to move frequently between different neighbourhoods and attractions.

### You're only making a few journeys

If you're mainly travelling between your accommodation and a small number of attractions, a standard ticket or contactless payment is often the simplest solution.

There's usually little benefit in purchasing a longer-duration pass if you'll only travel occasionally.

### You're staying near the M1 Red Line

Many visitors staying in **Sesto San Giovanni** use the metro several times throughout the day.

Depending on your itinerary, a daily or multi-day ticket can simplify your travel by reducing the need to purchase individual tickets before each journey.

## The main ticket options

Although ATM offers several fare products, most visitors only need to understand a small number of them.

### Standard ticket

A standard ticket is ideal if you're making occasional journeys during the day.

It's a practical option for visitors with flexible plans who don't yet know exactly how many trips they'll make.

The ticket can generally be used across Milan's integrated transport network according to its validity conditions.

### Daily pass

A daily pass may be convenient if you expect to travel frequently between attractions, restaurants, museums and your accommodation.

Instead of thinking about every individual journey, you can move around more freely during the period covered by the pass.

### Multi-day passes

If you're staying for several days and expect to use public transport regularly, a multi-day pass may be worth considering.

Whether it offers better value depends on your itinerary rather than the length of your stay alone.

### Contactless payment

Many visitors travel using a contactless bank card or compatible mobile wallet instead of purchasing a paper ticket.

This is often the easiest solution for short stays, provided you always use the same card or device throughout each journey.

## Where can you buy Milan public transport tickets?

Buying tickets is straightforward, and you'll find several convenient options throughout the city.

Tickets are commonly available from:

- ticket machines inside metro stations;
- authorised newsagents and retailers;
- the official ATM mobile app;
- selected transport hubs;
- supported contactless gates and validators.

If you're arriving at an airport or one of Milan's main railway stations, purchasing your first ticket is usually quick and clearly signposted.

Most metro ticket machines offer several language options, making them easy to use even if you don't speak Italian.

## How does contactless payment work?

For many visitors, **contactless payment** is the easiest way to travel around Milan.

Instead of purchasing a paper ticket, you can use a compatible bank card or mobile wallet wherever the contactless system is supported.

This option is particularly convenient if you:

- are staying for only a few days;
- prefer not to queue at ticket machines;
- arrive directly from an airport or railway station;
- don't know in advance how many journeys you'll make.

The most important rule is simple:

**Always use the same payment card or mobile device throughout your journey.**

A physical card and a mobile wallet connected to that card may be treated as different payment methods. Avoid switching between them during the same journey.

## Do you need to validate your ticket?

Yes, but the process depends on how you're travelling.

### Paper tickets

Paper tickets must be validated before or when starting your journey.

When travelling by metro, validation normally takes place as you pass through the station gates.

On buses and trams, use the onboard validation machine immediately after boarding.

Keep your ticket with you until the journey is complete, as you may need it during inspections or when leaving certain stations.

### Contactless payment

If you're travelling contactlessly, there is no paper ticket to validate.

Instead, tap the same payment card or mobile device wherever required by the system.

Following the same payment method consistently helps the network recognise your journey correctly.

## Choosing tickets for a full day of sightseeing

A day spent exploring Milan can involve more journeys than visitors initially expect.

For example, you might travel from your accommodation to the historic centre, continue to another neighbourhood for lunch, visit a museum in the afternoon and return after dinner.

Before choosing a daily pass, consider:

- how many separate journeys you're likely to make;
- whether most attractions are within walking distance of one another;
- whether you'll return to your accommodation during the day;
- whether you plan to visit destinations outside central Milan.

If you're spending most of the day in one compact area, individual tickets or contactless payment may still be enough.

If your itinerary includes several different districts, a pass may offer greater convenience.

## Local tip

> Don't buy the longest available pass automatically.

Many visitors assume that a pass is always the easiest or cheapest option. In practice, Milan's central attractions are often close enough to explore partly on foot.

Estimate how many times you'll actually use public transport before deciding.

A few simple habits can also make travelling easier:

- prepare your ticket before reaching the platform;
- keep paper tickets until the journey is complete;
- use the same card or device for contactless travel;
- check your return route if travelling late;
- confirm current fare rules before arriving.

## Frequently asked questions

### Which Milan public transport ticket is best for tourists?

The best option depends on how frequently you plan to travel.

For a small number of journeys, a standard ticket or contactless payment is usually sufficient. If you'll be using public transport frequently throughout the day, a daily or multi-day pass may be more convenient.

### Can I use the same ticket on the metro, buses and trams?

Milan has an integrated public transport system, so tickets can generally be used across the metro, buses and trams according to the applicable fare and validity rules.

Always check the conditions of the specific ticket you purchase.

### Can I use contactless payment on Milan public transport?

Contactless payment is available across much of Milan's transport network and is a convenient option for many visitors.

Use the same physical card or the same mobile device throughout your journey.

### Can two people travel using the same contactless card?

Each passenger should normally use a separate valid payment method or ticket.

Using one contactless card for several travellers may not register each passenger correctly.

### Do I need to buy a ticket before entering the metro?

If you're using a paper or digital ticket, purchase it before passing through the ticket gates.

If you're using contactless payment, tap your card or mobile device at the supported gate.

### Do paper tickets need to be validated?

Yes. Paper tickets must be validated according to the transport mode you're using.

Metro gates normally validate the ticket when you enter. On buses and trams, use the onboard validator after boarding.

### Where can I check current ticket prices?

Ticket prices, fare zones and available products may change.

Check the latest information published by **ATM Milano** before travelling, particularly if you're purchasing a multi-day pass or travelling beyond central Milan.
