## Milan Public Transport Tickets: Which One Should You Buy?

Whether you're arriving at the airport, travelling into the city centre or staying for several days, choosing the right public transport ticket in Milan is usually straightforward.

For most visitors there are only three realistic options:

- a standard ticket for occasional travel;
- a day pass if you'll make several journeys;
- contactless payment using your bank card.

This guide explains when each option makes sense, helping you avoid paying for more than you need.

{/* rx:audience */}

## Who is this guide for?

This guide is useful if you are:

- visiting Milan for the first time;
- staying for a weekend or short holiday;
- planning to use the metro, trams or buses;
- wondering whether you need to buy a paper ticket.

{/* rx:key-points */}

## Key points

- Most visitors only need a standard ticket, a day pass or contactless payment.
- The same tickets work across the metro, trams and buses within the urban network.
- Contactless payment is often the simplest option for short stays.
- Children and special tickets follow different rules, so check ATM's latest guidance if they apply to your trip.

## Which ticket should you choose?

### Standard ticket

The standard urban ticket is the best choice if you expect to make only a few journeys during the day.

Typical examples include:

- travelling from your accommodation to the city centre;
- visiting one or two attractions;
- returning in the evening.

For many tourists, this is all that's needed.

### 24-hour or multi-day passes

If you plan to travel frequently, a pass can be more convenient than buying individual tickets.

A pass is often worthwhile if you expect to:

- move between several neighbourhoods;
- use public transport throughout the day;
- rely on the metro for most of your sightseeing.

Choose the pass based on the length of your stay rather than trying to calculate every individual journey.

### Contactless payment

Many visitors no longer need to buy a physical ticket.

Simply tap your contactless bank card or compatible smartphone at the metro gates.

For short visits this is often the quickest and easiest solution because there is no need to queue at ticket machines.

## Where can you use these tickets?

Urban tickets are generally valid across Milan's integrated public transport network, including:

- Metro
- Trams
- Buses

Most visitors will spend almost all of their trip within this network.

If you're travelling beyond Milan, for example to Lake Como or other regional destinations, different tickets may be required.

{/* rx:local-area */}

## Buying tickets

You can usually buy tickets:

- at metro station ticket machines;
- from authorised retailers;
- through the official ATM app;
- or avoid buying one altogether by using contactless payment where available.

Ticket machines offer several languages and are easy to use, even if it's your first visit to Milan.

{/* rx:day-trips */}

## Travelling beyond Milan

Urban ATM tickets are designed for travel within Milan's public transport network.

If you're planning day trips to destinations such as Monza, Lake Como or Lecco, you'll normally need separate regional train tickets.

Read our dedicated day trip guides before travelling outside the city.

{/* rx:local-tip */}

## Local tip

If you're staying near the M1 Red Line, you may only need a small number of journeys each day.

Many visitors automatically buy a day pass, but a standard ticket or contactless payment is often the more economical choice for a typical sightseeing itinerary.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I use the same ticket on the metro, trams and buses?

Yes. Standard urban tickets are generally valid across Milan's integrated public transport network within the applicable fare area.

### Is contactless payment a good option for tourists?

For many short stays, yes. It removes the need to buy paper tickets and is often the fastest way to access the metro.

### Should I buy a multi-day pass?

Only if you expect to travel frequently throughout the day for several consecutive days. Otherwise, standard tickets or contactless payment may be better value.

### Do I need a different ticket for airports?

Airport journeys may follow different fare rules depending on the airport and the transport service you choose. Check the dedicated airport guide before travelling.
