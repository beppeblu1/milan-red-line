## What daily life feels like

Unlike the historic centre of Milan, Sesto San Giovanni is a place where everyday life continues at its own pace.

Residents shop at local supermarkets, stop for breakfast before work, meet friends for an evening aperitivo and use the metro as part of their daily routine.

For visitors, this means easier access to everyday services, quieter evenings and a more relaxed atmosphere after a busy day exploring Milan.

Rather than feeling like a tourist district, the area offers a more authentic experience while remaining closely connected to the city.

## Everyday essentials

One of the advantages of staying in Sesto San Giovanni is that most everyday services are within easy walking distance.

### Supermarkets

Whether you need breakfast supplies, ingredients for dinner or just a few essentials, you'll find several supermarkets nearby, including **Esselunga**, **Coop**, **Lidl** and **Carrefour**.

This makes longer stays particularly convenient, especially if you're travelling as a family or prefer preparing some meals yourself.

### Pharmacies

Several pharmacies are located throughout the neighbourhood, making it easy to buy medicines or other everyday essentials if needed.

### Local market

Every **Saturday**, Via **Puricelli Guerra** hosts the local street market.

Residents come here to buy fresh fruit and vegetables, clothing and a wide range of everyday products. It's also a pleasant way to experience the neighbourhood beyond the usual tourist routes.

## Food & coffee

You won't need to travel into central Milan for every meal. Sesto San Giovanni offers plenty of everyday dining options within walking distance.

### Breakfast

The neighbourhood has several cafés and pastry shops where locals stop for breakfast. Whether you prefer a quick espresso at the counter or a relaxed cappuccino with a fresh pastry, you'll find plenty of choice nearby.

### Quick lunch

If you're looking for something simple before catching the metro or after a day exploring Milan, you'll find bakeries, sandwich shops, kebab restaurants and casual cafés throughout the area.

### Pizza & Italian restaurants

There are several well-rated pizzerias together with traditional Italian restaurants serving seafood, grilled meat and regional dishes, making it easy to enjoy dinner without travelling into the city centre.

### International cuisine

If you're in the mood for something different, the neighbourhood also offers international restaurants, including a popular ramen restaurant alongside other Asian dining options.

### Gelato

During the warmer months, a couple of artisan gelaterias are just a short walk away and make an excellent stop after dinner or before an evening stroll.

### Aperitivo

Several bars around Sesto Rondò offer a relaxed aperitivo atmosphere with drinks and light snacks. They are popular with local residents and provide a quieter alternative to the busier nightlife districts in central Milan.

## Safety

### Is Sesto San Giovanni safe?

This is one of the questions visitors ask most often.

Like any large urban area, it's sensible to take normal precautions, especially around busy transport hubs or late at night. However, for most visitors, Sesto San Giovanni feels like a typical residential town where people commute, shop and go about their daily lives.

During the day the neighbourhood is generally busy with local residents, and the main streets around the metro stations are well frequented.

As with any destination, it's good practice to keep an eye on your belongings, stay aware of your surroundings and follow the same common-sense precautions you would use in any European city.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Sesto San Giovanni safe for tourists?

For most visitors, yes. It is a residential area where people live and work every day. As in any city, normal precautions are recommended, particularly around busy stations and late at night.

### Can I visit Milan without a car from Sesto San Giovanni?

Absolutely. The M1 Red Line provides direct access to central Milan, while regional trains make it easy to continue towards destinations such as Monza and Lake Como.

For more information, read our guides on [Where to Stay in Milan Without a Car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) and [How to Use Milan Public Transport](/guides/how-to-use-milan-public-transport).

### Are there supermarkets nearby?

Yes. Several supermarkets, including Esselunga, Coop, Lidl and Carrefour, are available within the neighbourhood.

### Where can I have breakfast?

There are numerous cafés and pastry shops throughout Sesto San Giovanni, making it easy to enjoy a traditional Italian breakfast close to your accommodation.

### Is there a local market?

Yes. Every Saturday, Via Puricelli Guerra hosts a popular neighbourhood market selling fresh food, clothing and everyday household products.

### Can I find restaurants without going into Milan?

Yes. The area offers a wide choice of pizzerias, Italian restaurants, cafés, gelaterias and international cuisine, so there's no need to travel into the city centre for every meal.

### Good to know

Most shops, supermarkets and cafés follow regular opening hours, although some independent businesses may close for part of the afternoon or on certain days of the week.

Sesto San Giovanni has its own tourist tax, which is separate from the City of Milan's tourist tax. Rates and exemptions may change over time, so it's always worth checking the latest information before your stay.

Public transport services, travel times and routes may also vary because of maintenance, strikes or special events. Before travelling, check the latest information from **ATM Milano** and the relevant railway operator if you're following a fixed schedule.
