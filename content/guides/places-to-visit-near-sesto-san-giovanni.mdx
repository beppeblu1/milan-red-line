## What can you visit nearby?

North Milan combines culture, architecture, green spaces and entertainment within a relatively compact area. These are some of the places most visitors enjoy exploring.

### Pirelli HangarBicocca

One of Europe's largest contemporary art spaces, **Pirelli HangarBicocca** occupies a former industrial building that now hosts large-scale installations and temporary exhibitions.

The permanent installation *The Seven Heavenly Palaces* is one of its best-known highlights. If you're interested in exploring this part of Milan, our guide to [Where to Stay Near Bicocca University](/guides/where-to-stay-near-bicocca-university) also introduces the surrounding district.

### Museo Campari

Located in nearby Sesto San Giovanni, **Museo Campari** tells the story of one of Italy's most iconic brands through advertising, graphic design and industrial history.

It's an excellent stop for visitors interested in Italian design, communication and local heritage.

### Parco Nord Milano

One of the largest urban parks in the metropolitan area, **Parco Nord Milano** offers walking and cycling paths, open green spaces and quiet areas to relax after a day in the city.

It's particularly popular with local residents and families throughout the year.

### Villa Torretta

Originally built as a noble residence, **Villa Torretta** is one of the area's most elegant historic buildings and reflects the architectural heritage that predates Sesto San Giovanni's industrial development.

Although now integrated into a modern hotel complex, it remains an interesting landmark to admire while exploring the neighbourhood.

### Carroponte

During the warmer months, **Carroponte** becomes one of Milan's best-known outdoor venues for concerts, festivals and cultural events.

If you're visiting for a performance, our guide to [Where to Stay Near Carroponte](/guides/where-to-stay-near-carroponte) explains why Sesto San Giovanni is one of the most convenient areas to stay.

## Why explore North Milan?

Unlike the historic centre, North Milan offers a quieter atmosphere where contemporary architecture, former industrial sites and green spaces coexist within a few kilometres of one another.

The area is easy to explore using the **M1 Red Line** and local public transport, making it possible to combine several attractions during the same day without spending hours travelling.

If you're travelling without a car, our guide to [Where to Stay in Milan Without a Car](/guides/where-to-stay-in-milan-without-a-car) explains why staying close to the metro can make exploring both Milan and North Milan much easier.

## Exploring the area

One of the advantages of staying in **Sesto San Giovanni** is that these attractions are located close enough to be combined into a flexible itinerary.

You might spend the morning exploring a contemporary art exhibition, enjoy lunch in the Bicocca district, relax in **Parco Nord Milano** during the afternoon and finish the day with a concert at **Carroponte**.

Unlike central Milan, where moving between attractions often means crossing the city, North Milan offers a more compact and relaxed experience.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I visit these attractions without a car?

Yes. Public transport is the easiest way to reach almost all of them, especially if you're staying near the M1 Red Line.

### Is Pirelli HangarBicocca free?

The permanent collection is generally free to visit, although temporary exhibitions may have different admission policies. Always check the official website before travelling.

### Is Parco Nord Milano suitable for families?

Yes. The park offers large green spaces, walking paths and playgrounds, making it popular with both residents and visitors.

### Is Carroponte open all year?

Carroponte mainly hosts concerts and events during the warmer months. The programme changes every season.

### Are these attractions far from central Milan?

No. Most can be reached quickly from both central Milan and Sesto San Giovanni using public transport.
